Nouns and articles - Les noms et les articles

There are two types of nouns in French :

Common nouns (les noms communs). Most of the time, they are preceded by an article.

Proper nouns (les noms propres), starting with an uppercase.

Common nouns

Grammatical gender

Some languages feature grammatical genders. French is one of them, and nouns in french can either be feminine or masculine. Knowing the gender of a noun is important, because a lot of words in the sentence that relate to that noun change depending on it's gender. There is no magic/logic here, you have to learn the gender with the noun.

Plural form

Nouns also have a plural form !

But it's relatively easy to determine the plural form of a noun. Let's take a word we've seen in the previous lesson : un chien. How to get the plural form of this word ? Simply add an S at the end of the word : des chiens. As you noticed, the article before the noun has changed, we'll talk about this soon. Most of the time, that's how to make the plural form of nouns.

The S in chiens is completely silent, the pronounciation of the singular and plural form are exactly the same.
